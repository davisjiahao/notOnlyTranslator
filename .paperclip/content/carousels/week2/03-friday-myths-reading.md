## Slide 2: Myth #1

**Visual:** Red "X" icon + Large text

**MYTH:** "I need to look up every word I don't know"

**The Belief:** Understanding 100% of the text is necessary for comprehension

**The Reality:** This kills your flow, destroys enjoyment, and leads to burnout

---

## Slide 3: Fact #1

**Visual:** Green checkmark icon + Large text

**FACT:** "Context clues give you 80% of what you need"

**The Science:** Research shows we comprehend text even with 10-15% unknown words

**Try This:** Guess the meaning first, look up only if it blocks understanding

---

## Slide 4: Myth #2

**Visual:** Red "X" icon + Large text

**MYTH:** "Reading translated texts helps me learn faster"

**The Belief:** Side-by-side translation accelerates vocabulary acquisition

**The Reality:** Your brain takes the easy route — you read the translation, not the English

---

## Slide 5: Fact #2

**Visual:** Green checkmark icon + Large text

**FACT:** "Struggle is where learning happens"

**The Science:** Productive struggle strengthens neural pathways and improves retention

**Try This:** Read at your level (i+1), use selective translation only for words above your level

---

## Slide 6: Myth #3

**Visual:** Red "X" icon + Large text

**MYTH:** "I should read classic literature to improve"

**The Belief:** Shakespeare and Dickens are "real" English

**The Reality:** Archaic vocabulary and complex syntax hinder, not help, modern learners

---
