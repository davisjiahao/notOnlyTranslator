## Slide 3: Meet the Solution

**Headline:** "i+1 Theory: The Science of Optimal Learning"

**Visual:** Simple equation: **i (your level) + 1 (just one step harder)**

**Source:** Developed by linguist Stephen Krashen in the 1970s

**Key Concept:** "Comprehensible Input" — material slightly above your current level

---

## Slide 4: Why It Works (The Science)

**Headline:** "Your Brain Loves a Challenge — But Not Too Much"

**Visual:** Brain illustration with flow state diagram

**Three Zones:**
1. **Comfort Zone** → No growth 🛑
2. **Learning Zone (i+1)** → Maximum growth 🌱
3. **Panic Zone** → Shutdown 😰

**The Sweet Spot:** 90-95% comprehension with 5-10% new words

---

## Slide 5: Real-World Example

**Headline:** "From 200 Words to 5,000: The i+1 Journey"

**Visual:** Timeline showing progression

**Week 1-4:** Reading graded readers (800-word vocabulary)
**Month 2-3:** News articles with 70% comprehension
**Month 4-6:** Native podcasts, TED talks
**Month 6+:** Fluent reading without translation

**Key:** Each step was just +10% harder than the last

---

## Slide 6: How to Apply It Today

**Headline:** "3 Steps to Find Your i+1 Level"

**Visual:** Checklist with 3 actionable items

**Step 1: Test Your Comprehension**
- Pick a text, count unknown words
- Target: 5-10 unknown words per 100

**Step 2: Adjust Your Input**
- Too easy? → Increase difficulty
- Too hard? → Simplify

**Step 3: Track Your Progress**
- Weekly vocabulary assessment
- Gradually increase difficulty
