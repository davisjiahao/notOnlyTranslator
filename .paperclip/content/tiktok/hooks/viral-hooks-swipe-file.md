## Hook Categories

### 1. Question Hooks (Curiosity-Driven)

**Direct Questions:**
- "Still translating every word you read?"
- "What if I told you you're reading English wrong?"
- "Why do smart English learners use this Chrome extension?"
- "How many tabs do you have open right now?"
- "Ever wonder why you plateau in vocabulary?"

**Rhetorical Questions:**
- "POV: You discovered selective translation"
- "What if reading in English didn't have to be hard?"
- "Imagine reading without Google Translate..."
- "What if your brain could learn while you read?"

**Challenging Questions:**
- "Is full translation actually hurting your English?"
- "Are you really learning, or just translating?"
- "Why do most language learners give up?"
- "Is your translation tool making you lazy?"

---

### 2. Statement Hooks (Authority/Revelation)

**Bold Claims:**
- "Stop translating everything."
- "This is how you actually learn vocabulary."
- "Google Translate is lying to you."
- "You're not learning English, you're memorizing translations."
- "This Chrome extension changed how I read English."

**Scientific/Authority:**
- "The REAL reason you plateau in vocabulary (science)"
- "Linguists discovered this 40 years ago..."
- "The i+1 theory explains everything."
- "Stephen Krashen was right about everything."

**Controversial/Unpopular Opinions:**
- "Unpopular opinion: Full translation slows you down."
- "Hot take: You don't need to memorize more words."
- "The vocabulary list method is outdated."
- "Flashcards are wasting your time."

---

### 3. Visual Hooks (Pattern Interrupt)

**POV Format:**
- "POV: You discovered selective translation"
- "POV: You can finally read English without crying"
- "POV: Your vocabulary after one month"
- "POV: You're reading the New York Times effortlessly"
- "POV: You found the perfect reading tool"

**Show, Don't Tell:**
- Screen recording: Before (50 tabs) → After (1 extension)
- Split screen: Frustrated reader → Confident reader
- Time-lapse: Reading speed comparison
- Before/After: Vocabulary level progression

**Visual Metaphors:**
- Training wheels for reading
- GPS for English
- Auto-translate vs. smart-translate
- Brain gym for vocabulary

## Hook Writing Formula

### Formula 1: Pattern Interrupt
```
[Expected thing] + [Unexpected twist]
Example: "Learning English? Stop memorizing words."
```

### Formula 2: Curiosity Gap
```
[What they know] + [What they don't know]
Example: "You know Google Translate. You don't know selective translation."
```

### Formula 3: Benefit + Proof
```
[Benefit] + [Specific result/timeframe]
Example: "Read 3x faster. Here's the proof."
```

### Formula 4: Relatable + Solution
```
[Pain point] + [Solution]
Example: "47 tabs open? This fixes everything."
```

## Best Practices

### Do's:
- ✅ Lead with emotion, follow with logic
- ✅ Use numbers when possible ("3x faster", "500 words")
- ✅ Make it about THEM, not about you
- ✅ Test hooks on friends first
- ✅ Keep it under 10 words when possible
- ✅ Use power words: "secret", "hack", "trick", "finally"

### Don'ts:
- ❌ Don't be clickbaity (must deliver on promise)
- ❌ Don't use jargon in the hook
- ❌ Don't make it too long
- ❌ Don't be boring
- ❌ Don't false promise
