## Video Script

### Scene 1: Hook (0-3 seconds)

**Visual:** Person holding up phone with confused expression
**Text Overlay:** "Why you plateau in vocabulary (science)"
**Audio:** Intriguing instrumental beat

**On-screen animation:** Graph showing flat line = plateau

---

### Scene 2: The Problem (3-12 seconds)

**Visual:** Whiteboard-style animation or simple graphics
**Text Overlay:** "The vocabulary plateau is real"

**Voiceover:**
> "Ever feel stuck at the same English level for months? You learn 10 new words, forget 10 old ones. That's the vocabulary plateau, and it's not your fault."

**Visual Elements:**
- Animation of person climbing stairs, then hitting a wall
- Flashcards with words flying away
- "2,000 words" counter stuck
- Frustrated face emoji

---

### Scene 3: The Science (12-25 seconds)

**Visual:** Clean infographic-style graphics
**Text Overlay:** "Stephen Krashen's i+1 Theory"

**Voiceover:**
> "Linguist Stephen Krashen discovered the secret. It's called the i+1 theory. 'i' is what you already know. '+1' is just one step above. Your brain learns best when it understands MOST of what it reads, but encounters a LITTLE that's new."

**Visual Elements:**
- Circle labeled "i" = known content
- Small ring around it labeled "+1" = new content
- Green arrow showing growth
- Text: "Comfort zone ❌ | Panic zone ❌ | Learning zone ✅"

**Key Graphics:**
```
[Comfort Zone]    [Learning Zone]     [Panic Zone]
i (known)         i+1 (known + 1)     i+10 (too hard)
😴 Too easy        😊 Just right        😰 Overwhelmed
```

---

### Scene 4: The Application (25-35 seconds)

**Visual:** Screen recording of NotOnlyTranslator
**Text Overlay:** "NotOnlyTranslator applies i+1 automatically"

**Voiceover:**
> "NotOnlyTranslator is built on this science. You set your current level, and it only shows translations for that '+1' - words just above your level. Everything else stays in English so you stay in the learning zone."

**Visual Elements:**
- Settings screen showing level selector
- Article with highlighted words matching the level
- Animation: "Your level: Intermediate" → Highlights 8% of words
- Comparison: Full translation vs. Selective translation

**Screen Demo:**
1. Open settings
2. Show CEFR levels (A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2)
3. Select B1 level
4. Navigate to article
5. Show ~5-10% of words highlighted (the +1)
6. Click to translate, then mark as known

---

### Scene 5: The Result (35-42 seconds)

**Visual:** Before/After comparison with real stats
**Text Overlay:** "Real results from real users"

**Voiceover:**
> "Users report learning 3x more vocabulary because they're finally reading at their i+1 level. Not too easy. Not too hard. Just right."

**Visual Elements:**
- Split screen: "Traditional method" vs "i+1 method"
- Stats overlay:
  - "+150 words/month" (traditional) vs "+450 words/month" (i+1)
  - "30 min/day studying" vs "Just reading normally"
- Happy reader animation

---

### Scene 6: CTA (42-45 seconds)

**Visual:** Back to host, pointing to bio
**Text Overlay:** "Link in bio to try it free"

**Voiceover:**
> "Try the i+1 method. Link in bio. Free."

**End Cards:**
- Logo + tagline: "Read at your level"
- "Follow for more language learning science"
- Thumbs up + subscribe gesture
