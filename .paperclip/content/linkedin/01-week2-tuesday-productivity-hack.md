### Post Content

```
The hidden productivity cost of reading in a second language:

I used to spend 40+ minutes on a 10-minute article.

Not because I'm a slow reader.
Because I was constantly context-switching.

🔍 Look up a word in a new tab
📖 Check the definition
🤔 Try to remember the context
🔙 Navigate back to the article
⏰ Repeat 20+ times

Each switch costs ~23 seconds of cognitive recovery time (Microsoft Research).
That's 8+ minutes of pure mental overhead on a single article.

Here's what changed:

Instead of translating everything or nothing, I started translating only what I actually don't know.

The result?
- 70% reduction in context switches
- Actually retained vocabulary (in-context learning)
- Reading became enjoyable again

The best part: my vocabulary improved faster because I wasn't overwhelmed by every unknown word.

For professionals who read English content regularly, this approach is a game-changer.
```
